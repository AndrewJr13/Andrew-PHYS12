<br> <br> <head>Class 5: 3D Printing</head> I was very excited for this weeks class. I have always been intrested in 3D printing but often didn't know how to start. For this week's class I started by doing a very short 10 minute print. I designed a hollowed out cube in Fusion 360 and uploaded it to the Prusa slicer. At first 3D design on Fusion seemed tough. I was very used to doing sketches in a single plane and then extruding them to a specific width. This time I had to do sketches in different planes, join pieces, and make cuts as well. Here's how the cube design came out. ![](./cube.png) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; ![](./cubo.png) After the print was finished, the cube had a lot of supports in it. The supports were necessary since most of the shape was hollowed out. I was able to use a pair of small pliers to extract the supports. It took a good amount of time and some supports did not come off easily but I was able to complete the shape. After this, I did a more lengthy print. I decided to make a component for my final project. Of course the component wouldn't be full size since the full size print would take around 4-5 hours. I made a smaller version that only took about 40 minutes. The door would be 3D because of the small handle on it but I also added something else. My final project will have a locking mechanism on it so I decided to prototype the slot that the locking mechanism would go into. I made a small cut in Fusion 360 and uploaded the design to the slicer. I then waited the 40 minutes for the print to finish and it came out very nicley.
