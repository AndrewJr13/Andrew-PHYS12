I wanted to go with trying to cut out a car logo. I found that a Mercedes logo would be simple enough but satisfactory in terms of its looks. All it took was a quick save of the image and an upload to fusion and I had the logo designed. ![](./merc.png) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; ![](./sVG.png) Next it was time to use Aspire and the shopbot. Aspire proved to be quite complicated at the first look. It was much different than Fusion in the sense that the software mainly used vector graphics to simulate the path that the drill bit would take. This proved to be complicated when making toolpaths for engraving and cutting. I was able to follow the quick tutorial posted on the main website and I was able to get it done. I used the cut toolpath to map out the frame of the logo then used an engrave toolpath to make the actual logo. Within about 20 minutes, I was able to handle the program. I then saved my toolpaths and prepared to cut. <br> <br> Update 7/18: Shopbot had various problems throughout lab session.
