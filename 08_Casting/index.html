We started out with a few demos in class on how to run the Shopbot and the wax machine. We then moved on to molding and casting with chocolate, metal, etc. <br> <br> <head>Shopbot and Aspire</head> <br> For my assignment I decided to start out with the Shopbot. I wanted to go with trying to cut out a car logo. I found that a Mercedes logo would be simple enough but satisfactory in terms of its looks. While the design for this logo would've been pretty simple, I was able to find a svg image to upload into fusion. I will provide a link for this image at the bottom of the page. All it took was a quick save of the image and an upload to fusion and I had the logo designed. ![](./merc.png) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; ![](./sVG.png) Next it was time to use Aspire and the shopbot. Aspire proved to be quite complicated at the first look. It was much different than Fusion in the sense that the software mainly used vector graphics to simulate the path that the drill bit would take. This proved to be complicated when making toolpaths for engraving and cutting. I was able to follow the quick tutorial posted on the main website and I was able to get it done. I used the cut toolpath to map out the frame of the logo then used an engrave toolpath to make the actual logo. Within about 20 minutes, I was able to handle the program. I then saved my toolpaths and prepared to cut. <br> <br> Update 7/18: Shopbot had various problems throughout lab session. My plans for next lab session is to make the mold using molten metal. It will be a relativley easy process as all I have to do is heat up some metal and pour it into the mold. As of right now, here are the initial pictures of the mold. ![](./mold.png) &nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp;&nbsp; ![](./mold1.png) <br> <br> <head>Update as of 7/20</head> <br> During this lab session I was finally able to get the shopbot up and working. With a few adjustments in Aspire and some quick adjustments to the machine itself, I was ready to try again. This time, I made sure to get an extremely precise reading on the thickness of the wood and then put that number into aspire. Next I adjusted the number of passes that the drill bit would make to 5 instead of 4 to ensure that the bit made it through the entire piece of wood. I also made the actual figure larger to prevent the drill from overlapping the engraving with the border of the shape. Finally, I made the engraving a few millimeters deeper just so it would look a bit better. Finally I took the finished toolpaths to the shopbot and was able to secure a sucess the first try.
